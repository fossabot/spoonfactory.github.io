Quality Assurance

At the moment I'm working as a Lead Quality Assurance Specialist at Solinor. Solinor is Software Company, which makes payment solutions from small projects to huge companies like Nets (former Luottokunta). There is only one PCI DSS certified Software Company in Finland and that is Solinor.

I starter at Solinor as a Software Developer. At the beginning it was hard to code in strict guidelines of PCI DSS but after I adopted them it was natural way of making things. Since that I don’t know any other way to make software than thru huge testing sets, security audits, peer programming, code / test reviews and continuous deployments.

Now at Solinor I’m creating testing guidelines, Continuous Deployment systems and sometimes working as a developer in different teams. As a PCI DSS certified company I need to follow rules of the certification. For example certification force to follow secure coding principles like OWASP TOP 10 and CWE/SANS TOP 25. Because of the OWASP and SANS I’m familiar with coding secure applications, white box testing and black box testing.

At Solinor we have always been using Scrum and Kanban in our daily work. We are also using user stories in all our tasks. Because of user stories we are moving from Test-driven development to behaviour-driven development in most cases. It makes sense to use natural written language to describe our tasks.

When I started at Solinor about three and half years ago the company was size of six developers. Now it is 30-employee company. It’s been fun and hugely challenging to work in Startup Company. When there is only bunch of workers in company they are all forced to learn each other’s work. I mean I started as developer but I needed to learn how to release my version of the application and how to maintain it. I have been in Solinor for about three years but I could say it’s 15 years working experience packed in three because of the steep learning curve.

Now I’m looking for new challenges. I want to know how things are done in most interesting company in Finland. I know how to make and test applications, which are transferring most of the Finnish credit card transactions. I have such a knowhow Supercell doesn’t want to miss. Contact me and lets talk more.

"My core knowhow is all kind of testing, continuous deployment and creating new innovative products. If I was to squeeze all my thoughts to one sentence I would say, my true passion is quality."

Ilari Mäkelä, Lead Quality Assurance Specialist at Solinor Oy
